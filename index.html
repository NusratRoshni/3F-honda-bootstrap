Frequently Asked Questions

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

Helmets in general need to be replaced 5 years after the date of manufacture ( NOT when you bought it) because of the natural deterioration of the materials that absorb the shock. If you have no idea when your helmet was made, check the inside of the helmet for details and get in touch with the seller or manufacturer if the seller isn't a biker store.

Make sure your brakes work. First do it off your bike, make sure the brake pads make contact with the rim or the rotor appropriately. Then do it with while riding your bike around the block. Make sure you test it with enough speed, but do it safely. It is common for cables to stretch and for air to build up in hydraulic brakes.

Always ride with at least one hand on the handlebars. Carry books and other items in a bicycle carrier or backpack.. Be on the lookout for hazards such as potholes, broken glass, gravel, puddles, leaves, and dogs. All these hazards can cause a crash. If you are riding with friends and you are in the lead, yell out and point to the hazard to alert the riders behind you.It is far more dangerous to ride at night than during the day because you are harder for others to see. If you have to ride at night, wear something that makes you more easily seen by others.
